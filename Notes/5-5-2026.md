- **Polarity of Molecules**: polarity of a bond shows how electrons are distributed between 2 atoms. Difference in electronegativity helps predict this. (See equation in notebook)
	- What is the polarity?
- Polar molecules: 
	- unequal distribution of electrons throughout the molecule.
	- Makes one end of the molecule electron rich (&-(diff symbol)) and the other end of the molecule electron poor (&+)
	- These molecules have polar bonds (vectors) that do not cancel (vector sum != 0)
	- have a preferred orientation in an electric field.
- Non-polar molecules:
	- have an equal distribution of electrons through out the molecule. Has electron rich (&-) and electron poor (&+) ends.
	- no preferred orientation in an electric field.
- The shape of CO_2 has two polar bonds. Since the visual shape is linear molecule the bond dipole vector sum is zero = polar bonds cancel. While H_2O has a bent molecule in its visual shape making it have a vector sum != zero. So the polar bonds do not cancel making it a polar molecule.
