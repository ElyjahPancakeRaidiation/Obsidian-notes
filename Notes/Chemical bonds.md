#Class_Notes #Definitions
A **chemical bond** is a combination of attractive and repulsive electric forces that glues atoms together to make molecules and polyatomic ions. Their almost like a strong springy stick that joins atoms. This "stick" also keeps atoms a certain typical distance apart called **bond length**. Another characteristic of this "stick" is **bond angle** meaning the bonded atoms can wiggle back and forth a bit. 

These wiggling motions are the basis of **molecular spectroscopy**.

Chemical bonds are extremely strong and usually break by the intense electric forces that act during a chemical reaction, by highly-energetic ultraviolet light, or by heating substances to 10,000C or more The amount of energy required to break a chemical bond is called the **bond energy**. It's typically 350 kJ/mol.
