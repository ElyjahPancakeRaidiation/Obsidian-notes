#Study_Notes #Definitions 

The mass percent or percent by mass of an element is the percentage of the mass of a sample of the compound that is that element. The mass percent of each element is the same for every size sample of compound no matter how large or small due to the [[Law of Constant Composition]].

## How to find the mass percent
- Example: $C r_(2)(C O_(3))_(3)$
Amount of moles of each element

| Element | Moles |
| ------- | ----- |
| Cr      | 2 mol |
| C       | 3 mol |
| O       | 9 mol |
Find the mass in grams of each element by multiplying by the molar mass of the element. 

| Element | Moles                             |
| ------- | --------------------------------- |
| Cr      | 2 mol * 51.9961 g/mol = 103.992 g |
| C       | 3 mol * 12.0096 g/mol = 36.0288 g |
| O       | 9 mol * 15.9994 g/mol = 143.995 g |
| Total:  | 284.0158 g                        |
Then we can divide their separated masses by the total mass in order to find the mass fraction of each element

| Element | Moles                             | Mass fraction                           |
| ------- | --------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Cr      | 2 mol * 51.9961 g/mol = 103.992 g | $(1 0 3 . 9 9 2 g)/(2 8 4 . 0 1 5 8 g)$ |
| C       | 3 mol * 12.0096 g/mol = 36.0288 g | $(3 6 . 0 2 8 8 g)/(2 8 4 . 0 1 5 8 g)$ |
| O       | 9 mol * 15.9994 g/mol = 143.995 g | $(1 4 3 . 9 9 5 g)/(2 8 4 . 0 1 5 8 g)$ |
| Total:  | 284.0158 g                        |                                         |
Final answer. Will usually be +/- 1 in the last digit

| Element | Moles                             | Mass % |
| ------- | --------------------------------- | ------ |
| Cr      | 2 mol * 51.9961 g/mol = 103.992 g | 37%    |
| C       | 3 mol * 12.0096 g/mol = 36.0288 g | 13%    |
| O       | 9 mol * 15.9994 g/mol = 143.995 g | 51%    |
| Total:  | 284.0158 g                        | 101%   |
