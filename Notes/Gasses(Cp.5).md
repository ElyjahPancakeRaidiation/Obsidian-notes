**Solid**: Particles are all attached and press on the bottom of the container
**Liquid**: Particles are attached but not in a rigid arrangement. Can take shape of the container.
**Gas**: Particles not in contact and in constant movement.

Pressure: Constantly produced from gas particles constantly colliding with the walls in a container.

FP = force of particles colliding with the walls
TS = total surface are of the container
$$ P r e s s u r e = (F P)/(T S) $$
**Kinetic energy** = $1 /2 m v^(2)$, v = velocity

### **Units for Pressure**:
- 1 mmHg = 1 torr
- 1 atmosphere = 1 atm = 760 mm Hg - 760 torr
- mmHg -> millimeters in mercury
- What is torr?
- Mercury always fell to the sea level resulting in 1 atm being 760 mmHg(simple explanation)

Gas has no volume, only mass. They are also in constant straight line motion except when they collide with each other or a wall.

Apart of the kinetic molecular theory: Particles do not attract or repel each other. Their collisions are elastic.
