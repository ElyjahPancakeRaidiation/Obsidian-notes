# **Acid and bases**
- Acids react with bases to make H_2O and an ionic compound and bases react with acids to make H_2O and an ionic compound.
- Pure water contains tiny but equal amounts of  hydronium($H_3O^+$) and hydroxide($O(H^-)$) ions. 
- Acid reacts with $H_2O$ increase concentration of $H_2O^+$
- Base increases concentration of $O(H^-)$ in water
## Acids
- Molecular substance that ionize(?) in water.
- *Strong* acids ionize essentially completely
- *Weak* acids ionize in equilibrium
- Strong acids: HCI, HBr, HI, $H N O_(3)$, $H C I O_(4)$, $H_(2)S O_(4)$(1st H^+ only), All other acids are weak. Strong acids get one arrow in their equations while weak acids get an arrow going back and fourth between the two compounds. 
- **Monoprotic acids** can transfer only $1 H^+$
- **Diprotic acids** can transfer $2 H^+$
- **Triprotic acids** can transfer $3H^+$
- Naming is very different for acids
	- **Binary acids**: aqueous solution of binary...?
		- Name = hydro-stem of name of other element-ic acid
			- Pure substance: HCI (g) = hydrogen Chloride
			- Aqueous solution: HCI(aq) = hydrochloric acid, $H_(2)S$(aq) = hydrosulfuric acid
	- **Oxo-acids**: molecular compound with some non-metal and some # of oxygen atoms on them
		- stem of name of non-metal - suffix acid
			- suffix = -ous = fewer O atoms, ...

## Gas forming reactions
- acid + carbonate or bicarbonate -> $C O_(2)$ gas
- acid + sulfite or bisulfite -> $S O_(2)$ gas
- acid + sulfide -> $H_(2)S$ gas
