#Study_Notes #Definitions 
**Electronegativity** is the amount of control an atom exerts over nearby bonding electrons when the atom is forming a chemical bond to another atom. Atoms with higher electronegativity exert more control, and atoms with a lower electronegativity exert less. Below is the periodic table and each elements electronegativity (there are no units for it).
![[Pasted image 20260411153543.png]]
Behavior of electronegativity:
- metal elements tend to have a much lower electronegativity than nonmetal elements.
- electronegativity increases as you go from left to right in the Periodic Table, along a period.
- electronegativity decreases as you go from top to bottom in the Periodic Table, down a group.
- Hydrogen has an exceptionally high electronegativity for a Group 1A element, which is why it alone in group 1A is classified as a nonmetal.
- Fluorine and oxygen are the most electronegative elements.
- When two atoms are bonded the more electronegative the atom is it will have slightly more of a negative charge to it causing the other atoms to have a more positive charge.
